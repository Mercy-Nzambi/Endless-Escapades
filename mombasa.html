Memories and Milestones: Touring Mombasa

written by: Mercy Nzambi published on: 15th November 2023

Mombasa is a coastal town in Kenya. Its among one of the most sought after tourist destinations by both the locals and foreigners, this is mostly because of the warm sandy beaches and also the many sites that are history-ladden and visually appealing. There are many places to site see and they include:

  1. Fort Jesus
  2. Haller Park
  3. Mamba Village
  4. Wild Waters
  5. Nyali Beach
  6. Mombasa Marine National Park

Fort Jesus

The Fort Jesus Museum was built by the Portuguese. It is a historical monument that displays various artefacts from the era where Mombasa served as a transit point for the slave trade and commodities. If one wishes to visit the place , you can request for a tour guide or decide to take a stroll by yourself.

Haller Park

Haller park is a product of Bamburi Cement's effort to convert barren landscape of limestone quarries into vibrant and diverse ecosystem of forests, grasslands and ponds. It comprises of:

  1. A Wildlife Sanctuary
  2. Animal Orphanage
  3. Warthog Boma
  4. Reptile Park
  5. Forest Walk

Haller park is a beautiful place to visit especially for the nature lovers.

Mamba Village

Is located in Nyali and is home to the largest crocodile farm in Africa. One can also enjoy the delicacy of the crocodile meat!

Wild Waters

Ut is a family entertainment park that boasts of 15 adult and kids waterslides, a radiant disco arena, a food court, a gaming arcade, bumper cars and a number of theme rides.

Nyali Beach

There is no travellimg without having visited the sandy beaches. Nyali beach is a clean and breezy beach and one can enjoy the boat rides and surfing on he waves of Indian Ocean. Let's not forget the famous "madafu" that one gets to enjoy drinking while enjoying the scenic view of Indian Ocean as the wave hit the coast and the breeze hitting one's face.

Mombasa Marine National Park

It is home to a colorful variety of marine species of crabs, starfish, stonefish, corals, turtles and sea grasses.
